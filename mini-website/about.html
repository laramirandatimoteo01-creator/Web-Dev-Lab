What is Climate Change?

Climate change refers to the long-term alteration of Earthâ€™s climate patterns, primarily caused by human activities such as burning fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial processes. These activities release greenhouse gases like carbon dioxide and methane into the atmosphere, trapping heat and leading to global warming.

The effects of climate change are already being felt worldwide: rising sea levels, melting glaciers, extreme weather events, and threats to biodiversity. Understanding the causes and consequences of climate change is the first step toward taking meaningful action to protect our planet.
